Muted Destiny

.......As I sat on my bed, looking up at the ceiling like I was in an examination hall clueless about what to write, actually here, in reality, I was clueless of what next, though my flesh was satisfied as always, my inner man was yearning for survival.
"Thank you, sir, I enjoyed myself to the full. You are the best”. My weakness said to me as she left my room to her room next door.
Kunle, you don’t need all these things you are doing, remember who you are, this life you are living won’t take you anywhere".
Those are words of Wande, my longtime friend, who isn’t fed up with advising me, he told me one day when she caught my weakness in bed with me. Since that day, those words keep ringing in my heart, and that moment wasn't an exemption. Okay, Wait! Let me introduce myself.
My name is Kunle Donald, the first child and only son of Pastor and late Lady Evangelist Oluwafunto Donald of Jehova Nissi Gospel Church.
I am a young naïve 18 years old boy, at least to my dad, I am still innocent, but the actual reality is different.
I am a first-class student; this has allowed me to have my way with almost all my female classmates. Yes, classmates, Ladies flock around me, like birds.
Though I don’t like all the attention that came with my brilliancy, that’s my weakness. Let’s take a pause here. Already tired, I prayed to God for forgiveness, I know God must be tired of hearing me asking for forgiveness always.
But I persist in turning back to God. After the prayer, I slept off, I saw a young man in my dream, he always come to have a word with me.
“My young friend,” as he always call me, “why don’t you face your weakness, you are born for greatness, and your destiny needs nurturing, but this weakness might hinder you from reaching your peak and might cause your destiny to be muted, your light might turn to darkness soon if you aren’t careful”.
He said in finality as he looked me in the face.
“I have heard you sir, but you need to help me” I replied him with an expectant heart. "I will surely visit you later my young friend, I will answer your question from there." He said in finality.
The ringing tone coming from my phone draws me away from my dream. I sluggishly picked the phone and took it to my ear with one of my eyes still closed, I wish we could talk more.
His words are always comforting. Bro Kunle, where are you, have you forgotten you are taking the opening prayer? Immediately, I stood up from the bed, still confused, “eh hmmm, Bro Theophilus, I don’t know what you are talking about” I replied to him, still confused.
